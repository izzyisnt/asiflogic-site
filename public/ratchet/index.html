The talin–vinculin ratchet

Calcium is cleared in seconds and Piezo1 inactivates in milliseconds — neither can remember mechanical history. The cell integrates it with a molecular ratchet: myosin loads talin, a domain unfolds, and vinculin captures it (the pawl) — a click that can’t slip back. Whether a click is caught is a race between unfolding and the clutch slipping, and that race is what reads stiffness.

myosin (pulls) talin rod (the rack) vinculin (the pawl) actin (the anchor)

Reading it. Myosin pulls the talin rod upward; each attempt tries to advance one tooth. On stiff matrix force builds fast, talin unfolds before the clutch slips, and vinculin catches the tooth — the ratchet clicks forward and locks, accumulating an irreversible reinforcement. On soft matrix the load builds too slowly, the clutch slips first, and the rod springs back — no capture. With Tpm2.1 lost the actin anchor can’t bear load at all, so the rod never tensions and the ratchet freewheels — the cancer cell is stiffness-blind.
